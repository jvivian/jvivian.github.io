I am a fourth-year bioinformatics Ph.D. student at UC Santa Cruz working in the Computational Genomics Lab at the Genomics Institute. I spent the first two years helping develop Toil, an open-source distributed workflow system designed for massive scalability. This work enabled a large-scale compute of gene- and isoform-level expression values from 20,000 RNA-seq samples across several important datasets, which you can read about here. This work used an RNA-seq workflow I devloped and maintain.

My current PhD research is focused on analyzing the expression differences between a variety of cancers at the tissue level. I believe in open-source software and science and actively share my work on Github.
