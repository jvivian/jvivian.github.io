I am a third-year Bioinformatics Ph.D. student at UC Santa Cruz working in the Computational Genomics Lab at the Genomics Institute. I spent the first two years developing Toil, an open-source distributed workflow system designed for massive scalability, and genomic workflows that use Toil. This work culminated in a large-scale compute of gene- and isoform-level expression values across 20,000 RNA-seq samples from several important datasets, which you can read about here.

My current PhD research is focused on analyzing the expression differences between a variety of cancers at the tissue level. I believe in open-source software and science and actively post all my work on Github.
