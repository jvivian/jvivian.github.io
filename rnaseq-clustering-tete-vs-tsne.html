t-SNE is a relatively new dimensionality reduction algorithm that has garnered popularity for its ability to create beatiful 2-dimsenional clusters of iconic datasets like MNIST. t-SNE has recently caught the eye of the genomics community, who often deal in high-dimensional feature spaces, but has several pitfalls that a lot of users are unaware of, such as cluster size and distance between clusters being typically uninformative. Source: distill.pub/2016/misread-tsne

t-ETE is an alternative algorithm actively in development at UCSC that aims to address t-SNE's shortcomings and provide dimesnionality reduction that emphasizes information retention over aesthetics.
Low-dimensional Data Embedding via Robust Ranking

This first example is expression data collected from both normal and tumorous thyroid tissue. Both t-SNE and t-ETE identify two subpopulations in the GTEx thyroid data, but t-SNE places the two clusters on opposite sides of the tumor data. Dimensionality reduction is often used iteratively, where subclusters are then selected to be replot. If we isolate just the thyroid tissue from GTEx (blue) and recluster it with t-SNE and t-ETE, how much of the structure is preserved?

t-SNE turns the samples into a single blob whereas t-ETE preserves the original structure.
